About Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is a stunning white marble mausoleum located on the southern bank of the Yamuna River in the city of Agra, in the northern Indian state of Uttar Pradesh.
The Taj Mahal was commissioned in 1632 by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan, who reigned from 1628 to 1658. It was built in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal, who died in 1631 during childbirth. The Taj Mahal is not just a monument; it is a symbol of Shah Jahan's undying love for Mumtaz Mahal .


Attractions

The Main Mausoleum

The central structure of the Taj Mahal is a grand marble mausoleum built in memory of Mumtaz Mahal, the beloved wife of Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan. The mausoleum is renowned for its symmetrical architecture, white marble facade, and intricate inlay work using semi-precious stones.

The Charbagh Gardens

Description: The Taj Mahal is surrounded by lush, perfectly symmetrical gardens known as Charbagh. These Persian-style gardens are divided into four quadrants, representing the four rivers of paradise, and feature beautiful pathways, fountains, and lush greenery that enhance the serene atmosphere.

Reflection Pool

The Reflection Pool runs through the center of the gardens and reflects the magnificent image of the Taj Mahal. This pool provides a stunning view of the mausoleum, especially during sunrise and sunset, creating a mesmerizing sight for visitors.

Inlay Work and Calligraphy

The Taj Mahal is famous for its exquisite pietra dura inlay work, where semi-precious stones are intricately set into the marble to create floral and geometric patterns. The entrance arch and interior walls are adorned with detailed Islamic calligraphy that adds to the monument's grandeur.
