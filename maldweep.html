Geography:

Archipelago: The Maldives consists of approximately 1,200 coral islands grouped into 26 natural atolls, which are spread over about 90,000 square kilometers (34,750 square miles). Only around 200 of these islands are inhabited.

Climate:

Tropical Climate: The Maldives has a tropical monsoon climate, characterized by warm temperatures year-round, with an average temperature of around 30°C (86°F). Monsoons: There are two distinct seasons in the Maldives: the dry season (northeast monsoon) from November to April, and the wet season (southwest monsoon) from May to October, with heavier rainfall and higher humidity.

History and Culture:

Early Settlement: The Maldives has a rich history dating back over 2,500 years, with early settlers believed to be from southern India and Sri Lanka. The islands have been influenced by various cultures, including Indian, Sri Lankan, Arab, and African.
